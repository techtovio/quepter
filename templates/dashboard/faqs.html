Earning and Managing Points FAQs

Members can earn points through various activities on the platform, including:
  • Inviting other members: Each successful invite earns you 2 points, and the invited member receives 0.5 points.
  • Engagements on posts: For every 100 likes on your posts in the community forums, you earn 1 point.
  • Club Weekly Funding: For every KES 10 contributed to club funding, you earn 1 point.
  • Downloads of your Ebook: Each download of your uploaded ebook earns you 10 points.
  • Engaging with Promoted Social Media Accounts: The points you earn depend on the promotion amount associated with each account.
  • Participating in Leaderboard Tasks: The points you earn depend on the Task and the rewards amount.

You can transfer points to other members through the P2P Section. To do so, you need the Profile ID of the member you want to send points to. This ID can be found in the Profile section under "Accounts."

Yes, you can purchase points on the platform. The rate is KES 10 per point. The option to buy points is available in your profile's "Accounts" section.

You can list your accumulated points for sale in the P2P Section. A minimum of 10 points is required to list your points for sale, and a service fee of 1 point will be deducted for every 10 points listed.

Your Profile ID is available in the "Profile" section under "Accounts." Share this ID with other members who wish to send you points.

For every KES 10 contributed to the Club Weekly Funding, you earn 1 point. The more you contribute, the more points you accumulate, which can be used towards proposing projects and other activities on the platform.

Yes, you can earn points by engaging with promoted social media accounts. The amount of points earned depends on the promotion amount tied to each account. Details of the points offered are provided with each promotion.

Funding Opportunities Eligibility FAQs

To apply for funding on selected opportunities, a member must meet the following criteria:
  • Minimum Points: The member should have the required minimum points specified for the opportunity.
  • Community Membership: The member must have been part of the community for at least 2 months.
  • Club Membership: The member should be an active member of at least one club that is directly related to the funding opportunity, with a history of funding in that club at least 10 times.
  • Invitations: The member must have invited at least 10 other members to join the community.

Locations play a key role in some funding opportunities. For instance, agribusiness opportunities might be funded for members located in areas that are suitable for agricultural activities. Each opportunity may have specific location requirements, so members should check the criteria before applying.

Being an active member of a club ensures that you are engaged with the community and have a track record of contributing to club activities. This engagement is important as it demonstrates your commitment and participation in the area related to the funding opportunity, making you a more suitable candidate for funding.

If you do not have enough points to apply for a funding opportunity, you can accumulate more points through various activities, such as inviting new members, participating in community discussions, contributing to club funding, or buying points in the P2P section. Once you have the required points, you can apply for the opportunity.

Each funding opportunity will list its eligibility criteria, including the minimum points required, relevant club memberships, and location requirements. You can review these criteria on the opportunity’s details page to see if you qualify before applying.

Project Proposal FAQs

To propose a project, you need to meet the following criteria:
  • You must have accumulated at least 100 points.
  • You must have invited at least 10 new members to join the community.
  • You should be an active member of one of the clubs that directly relate to the category of the project you wish to propose.
  • You must have participated in the weekly club funding at least 10 times.
  • You must have been a member of the community for at least 2 months.

Joining a club that aligns with your project’s category ensures that you have the necessary community support and expertise relevant to your project's focus. It also fosters collaboration and ensures that your project proposal is well-supported by like-minded individuals.

To join a club, go to the "Clubs" section of the platform and select a club that matches your project’s focus area. Simply click "Join" and pay one time membership fee of KES 100 to become a member. Make sure the club you join aligns with the category of your proposed project.

Weekly club funding is a collaborative effort where club members contribute towards community projects and activities. Participating in this funding shows that you are actively engaged in the club and committed to community growth. You need to have participated at least 10 times to be eligible to propose a project.

You can view your funding participation history in the Club Participation section of your profile. This will show the number of times you've contributed to weekly funding efforts.

No, to propose a project, you need to have been a member of the Quepter Youth Hub community for at least 2 months. This ensures that members have ample time to familiarize themselves with the platform and engage with the community before proposing a new project.

If you do not meet the eligibility criteria, your project proposal will not be accepted. It’s important to ensure that you fulfill all requirements before submitting your proposal to improve your chances of approval.

There is currently no limit to the number of projects you can propose, as long as you meet the required criteria for each proposal. However, you must wait for the approval or rejection of an ongoing project before submitting a new one.

Choosing the Right Category for Your Club: FAQs

We offer a wide range of categories to accommodate different interests and expertise. These include Education & Learning, Business & Entrepreneurship, Technology & Innovation, Creative Arts & Media, Health & Wellness, Sports & Recreation, Finance & Investment, and many more. This allows members to find a category that aligns with their passion and goals.

To select the best category for your club, consider the main purpose and activities of your club. For example, if your focus is on technology-related topics like AI, software development, or innovation, choose the "Technology & Innovation" category. If your club centers around helping the community, consider "Social Impact & Community Service" or "Environment & Sustainability." Always match your club's core mission with the most relevant category.

Yes, you can change the category of your club if you find that another category better represents your club's goals and activities. However, we recommend choosing your category carefully during the initial setup to ensure consistency in your club's growth and activities.

Consider the primary activities and focus areas of your club. For instance, if your club is centered on discussing financial literacy, investments, or cryptocurrency, then "Finance & Investment" would be the most suitable category. If your club promotes health practices, exercise routines, or mental wellness, "Health & Wellness" would be a better fit. Review the description of each category to see which aligns most closely with your club's mission.

Selecting the right category helps members with similar interests find your club more easily, increasing engagement and participation. It also ensures that your club is featured in relevant sections of the community, allowing you to connect with members who are most likely to benefit from and contribute to your club's activities.
